### Direct Contradictions — Legal Precedent Interpretation

1. **ICJ Nicaragua v. United States (1986) — Does it bar anticipatory self-defense?**
   - PRO claims: "Court left open possibility for imminent nuclear threats"
   - CON claims: "Court rejected all US self-defense claims without UNSC"
   - **Verdict**: ⚠️ PARTIALLY DISPUTED — Court explicitly rejected US self-defense in that case (Nicaragua had no nuclear program). Whether ICJ ruling categorically bars anticipatory self-defense in nuclear contexts vs. leaves it open is scholarly debate. Both sides cite same judgment but interpret precedent reach differently. No clear factual error by either side.

2. **Israel Osirak (1981) and al-Kibar (2007) — Do they constitute binding precedent?**
   - PRO claims: "Precedent tacitly accepted; no systemic collapse of IL"
   - CON claims: "Osirak was condemned; al-Kibar was unacknowledged; OMH is first public endorsement by superpower"
   - **Verdict**: ✅ BOTH FACTUALLY CORRECT but disagree on precedent weight. UNSC Res 487 did condemn Osirak. Israel never admitted al-Kibar until 2018. OMH in 2025 was publicly announced. Factual facts verified; disagreement is prospective/normative.
